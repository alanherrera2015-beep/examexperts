Exam Risk Self-Quiz

A 10-question self-assessment to identify your highest-risk areas before your next exam — so you know exactly where to focus your study time.

How to Use This Quiz
For each question, circle or tick the answer that best describes you right now. Tally your score at the end using the scoring guide. Be honest — the more accurate your answers, the more useful your results will be.

1. How many days before your exam do you typically start studying?

  • A) 4+ weeks in advance (0 pts)
  • B) 1–3 weeks before (1 pt)
  • C) Less than a week before (2 pts)
  • D) I cram the night before (3 pts)

2. When you study, which best describes your method?

  • A) I use practice questions and test myself regularly (0 pts)
  • B) I re-read notes and highlight key sections (2 pts)
  • C) I watch YouTube videos and hope it sticks (3 pts)
  • D) I haven't really found a method that works (3 pts)

3. How do you feel the night before a major exam?

  • A) Calm and prepared — I know what to expect (0 pts)
  • B) A little nervous but manageable (1 pt)
  • C) Stressed and still trying to cram more material (2 pts)
  • D) Panicking — I feel like I don't know anything (3 pts)

4. During the exam, how often do you experience anxiety that affects your performance?

  • A) Rarely — I stay focused (0 pts)
  • B) Sometimes, but I push through (1 pt)
  • C) Often — I blank out on things I studied (2 pts)
  • D) Almost always — I feel frozen or rush through (3 pts)

5. Have you failed or narrowly passed this exam (or a similar one) before?

  • A) No — first attempt (0 pts)
  • B) I passed but barely made it (1 pt)
  • C) Yes, I failed once (2 pts)
  • D) Yes, I have failed more than once (3 pts)

6. How well do you understand the exam format and question types you'll face?

  • A) Very well — I've done practice exams in that format (0 pts)
  • B) Somewhat — I've reviewed the format but not practiced it (1 pt)
  • C) Not really — I just study the content (2 pts)
  • D) Not at all — I didn't know I should look at the format (3 pts)

7. Do you have a clear, written study schedule for the time between now and your exam?

  • A) Yes — mapped out day by day (0 pts)
  • B) I have a rough idea but nothing written (1 pt)
  • C) Not really — I just study when I have time (2 pts)
  • D) No plan at all (3 pts)

8. How confident do you feel about your highest-risk topics right now?

  • A) Confident — I've tested myself and I know them (0 pts)
  • B) Somewhat — I recognize them but get questions wrong sometimes (1 pt)
  • C) Not confident — I still get confused by core concepts (2 pts)
  • D) I'm not sure what my highest-risk topics even are (3 pts)

9. How much is riding on the outcome of this exam for you?

  • A) It matters, but there are other options if I don't pass (0 pts)
  • B) It matters quite a bit for my grade or progress (1 pt)
  • C) It's critical — failing delays graduation or costs me money (2 pts)
  • D) Everything — failing ends my program or career path (3 pts)

10. How much time do you realistically have to study between now and your exam?

  • A) Plenty of time — 4+ weeks with consistent study hours (0 pts)
  • B) 2–4 weeks but my schedule is busy (1 pt)
  • C) About a week — it's tight (2 pts)
  • D) Less than a week or I'm not sure (3 pts)

📊 Score Your Results

Add up your points from all 10 questions and find your risk level below.

0–8 pts

Low Risk. You have solid habits in place. Focus on maintaining your schedule, drilling weak spots with practice questions, and managing exam-day nerves.

9–16 pts

Moderate Risk. Some gaps in your study approach or timing could cost you points. Review questions 2, 6, and 7 — those areas typically have the biggest ROI for improvement.

17–24 pts

High Risk. Multiple factors are working against you right now. You need a structured plan, exam-style practice, and ideally expert support before your test date. Don't wait — address this now.

25–30 pts

Critical Risk. Your situation is serious, but it is fixable. Students who score in this range often turn it around completely with structured mentoring focused on the right areas. This is exactly what Exam Experts was built for.
