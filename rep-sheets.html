The 5-Step Analogy Method

Our proprietary teaching sequence — from confusion to independent mastery in a single session.

What makes this different: Most tutoring starts at Step 3. We start at Step 1 — because a student who understands the concept will always outperform one who just memorized a procedure. Every Exam Experts tutor completes our proprietary Analogy Method Training.

🎈 Step 1 — Simple Analogy First

We open with a vivid real-world analogy so the concept "clicks" before a single definition is spoken. Understanding the why creates a mental hook that makes everything stick.

Example: "Think of ATP like a rechargeable battery — your cells spend it for energy and recharge it through respiration."

📖 Step 2 — The Real Definition

Once the concept is intuitive, we layer in the precise academic definition — exactly the language an exam expects. Students own both the intuition and the vocabulary needed to succeed.

🔬 Step 3 — We Demonstrate

Your tutor fully solves a representative exam problem out loud — narrating every step, every decision, and every reason. You see the expert thought process in real time.

🤝 Step 4 — You Try, With Support

You work through a similar problem while your tutor guides with Socratic prompts — steering your thinking, not giving answers. Mistakes become learning moments, not setbacks.

✅ Step 5 — Independent Mastery

You tackle a fresh problem completely solo — start to finish. When you can solve it independently and explain your reasoning, your tutor confirms you're exam-ready. We never move on until you've genuinely got it.

The Sequence at a Glance

  1. Analogy — concept clicks instantly
  2. Definition — academic language owned
  3. Demonstrate — expert model observed
  4. Guide — Socratic scaffolding applied
  5. Mastery — independent proof achieved

The Research Behind Our Method

Every step of our framework is grounded in peer-reviewed educational science.

Step 1 — Analogical Reasoning

Richland & Simms (2015, WIREs Cognitive Science) confirmed that structural analogies accelerate schema formation — the brain maps new concepts onto familiar frameworks, reducing cognitive effort and dramatically improving retention.

Step 2 — Precise Academic Language

Nagy & Townsend (2012, Reading Research Quarterly) demonstrated that explicit instruction in academic vocabulary is a core lever of disciplinary literacy — students who learn the exact language of a field outperform peers on high-stakes assessments.

Step 3 — Worked Examples Effect

Sweller, van Merriënboer & Paas (2019, Educational Psychology Review) updated 20 years of Cognitive Load Theory: fully-worked examples reduce extraneous cognitive load, freeing working memory to build transferable mental schemas.

Step 4 — Scaffolding & ZPD

Belland, Walker, Kim & Lefler (2017, Review of Educational Research) — a meta-analysis of 144 studies — found that structured scaffolding during problem-solving produces significant learning gains, especially when faded as competence grows.

Step 5 — Mastery Learning

Zimmerman & Dibenedetto (2008, Psychology in the Schools) showed that requiring students to demonstrate independent mastery before advancing leads to substantially better outcomes in high-stakes testing contexts.

Reinforcement Through Retrieval

Yang et al. (2021, Psychological Bulletin) — a meta-analysis of 222 experiments — confirmed that practice testing boosts long-term retention far beyond re-reading or passive review. Every session closes with active recall to lock in what was learned.
