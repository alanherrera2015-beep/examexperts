Our 5-Step Learning Framework

A research-informed progression that builds understanding before speed.

1) Analogy: connect new ideas to what students already know.
2) Definition: introduce precise language and core rules.
3) Demonstration: tutor models the skill with worked examples.
4) Guided Practice: student solves with coaching and immediate feedback.
5) Independent Mastery: student applies skills confidently alone.

Example: Physics

Problem: A 2 kg object accelerates at 3 m/s². Find net force.

1) Analogy: Think of pushing a shopping cart. A heavier cart needs more push, and a stronger push makes it speed up more. Force works the same way (mass × acceleration).

2) Definition: Newton’s 2nd law is F = ma, where F is net force, m is mass, and a is acceleration.

3) Demonstration: Substitute values: F = (2 kg)(3 m/s²).

4) Guided Practice: Multiply 2 × 3 to get 6; units combine to N (newtons).

5) Independent Mastery: Net force = 6 N.
